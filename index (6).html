§ 01 — Profile

About me

A young researcher who believes AI systems must be reliable, transparent, and secure before they can be considered truly intelligent.

I'm Abrar — an Information Systems undergraduate at Telkom University (GPA 3.67/4.00) with a deep interest in machine learning, model robustness under distribution shift, and the security of intelligent systems. I have built 20+ end-to-end projects that combine data analysis, secure authentication, and practical system design.

Beyond development, I teach — as a laboratory assistant for Data Mining, Computer Networks, and Information System Security at Telkom University, and as a coding & digital technology instructor at DigiKidz. Mentoring 100+ university students and dozens of younger learners has sharpened my communication skills — a capability essential for explaining models and validating research ideas.

My research spans spam text classification, medical image classification, cloud cryptography, Graph Neural Networks for cancer stage stratification, and studies of model stability under distribution shift. I am drawn to one question: how do we build ML systems that are not only accurate, but that we can still trust when the world changes?
